1) **집 전체 묘사**: I live in a three-bedroom apartment located in Suwon. It has two bathrooms, a cozy living room, a kitchen, a balcony, and a small laundry room. The apartment isn't very large, but it's just the right size for my family of four. One thing I really like about it is that it gets a lot of natural sunlight throughout the day, which makes the whole place feel bright and welcoming.

3) **마무리**: Overall, it's a warm and comfortable home where my family enjoys spending time together.

## Q2. [가족과의 활동 서술] Tell me about the activities that you and your family like to do at home. What do you usually do with your family on weekdays? What do you do on weekends?

### ==Answer==
1) **가족 소개**: I live together with my wife and two sons.
2) **주중**: On weekdays, my family members are usually too busy to spend much time together. My wife and I have work, and our sons go to school, so we only get to talk briefly during dinner or right before bed.

I take care of the rest, including cleaning the living room and taking out the trash, since I don't mind moving around and staying active. Our sons help by tidying up their rooms. Sometimes, handling house chores is tiring, but it's worth doing. After we finish all the chores, we often read books together or watch a movie on Netflix to unwind and enjoy each other's company.

## Q3. [어릴 적 집과 이후의 변화] Tell me about the house or the apartment where you grew up. What did the home or surrounding area look like when you were a child? How has it changed?

### ==Answer==
1) **어릴 적 집 묘사**: I grew up in a typical apartment building that was old and worn out, but it felt warm and had happy memories. We had simple and practical dark wood furniture. Although the space showed signs of age, it always felt safe and comfortable.
2) **변화**: I never went back to see the apartment or the neighborhood after moving out. The buildings might have been renovated or the neighborhood might have become more modern, but in my memory, it hasn't changed at all. I sometimes miss that old apartment.

## Q4. [가족과의 특별한 기억] Tell me about one special family memory that took place at home. Maybe something funny or surprising happened. Tell me about that day or the moment that you remember when you think about spending time with your family.

### ==Answer==
1) **배경**: When I was about 11, my dad gave me money to buy a cake for my mom's birthday.
2) **전개**: But on the way, I saw my friends playing arcade games and got completely distracted. One game led to another, and suddenly... the money was gone.
3) **절정**: I ran to the bakery, hoping for a miracle, but all I could buy was maybe a candle. I was too scared to go home, so I stood outside, feeling guilty. My dad eventually found me. He was upset, but he forgave me, saying it could happen to anyone.
4) **결말**: We didn't have a cake that day, but we still had a warm and happy birthday for my mom. And... Mom still teases me about it.

## Q2. [역할 수행 과정 서술] On a typical day, how do you handle your responsibilities?

### ==Answer==
1) **역할 소개**: As I mentioned, my responsibility at home is related to the trash and recycling, and I take a few steps to complete them.
2) **단계 1**: I start by sorting the trash right at home. We usually have separate bins for paper, plastic, glass, and food waste in the kitchen. Whenever I throw something away, I take a quick look to check if it needs to be rinsed or flattened.
3) **단계 2**: Second, I take the trash out to the designated area in my apartment complex. It is usually near the large trash station in front of my apartment.
4) **단계 3**: After that, I just make sure everything is placed in the right spot. If you put something in the wrong bin or use the wrong bag, the janitor might leave a sticker asking you to fix it.
5) **마무리**: Sometimes, all these steps are a bit annoying and time-consuming, but I think it's something we must do to protect the environment.

I had to sweep the floor, wipe down my desk, and make sure everything was in the right place. My parents believed it was important for me to keep my own space clean and organized. It wasn't always fun, but I knew it was my job, so I did my best.
2) **역할 2 (동생 돌보기)**: I was also expected to look after my younger brother when my parents were busy. I played with him, helped him with homework, and made sure he didn't get into trouble. It felt good to be trusted, and I learned how to be more responsible and helpful.
3) **마무리**: Looking back, those simple tasks helped me grow a lot.
