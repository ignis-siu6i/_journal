## Q1. [14번 유형: 재활용의 변화] Handling recyclables has been changed over time. What did recycling materials used to be? What is involved these days? How has recycling changed?

### ==Answer==
1) **도입**: When I was a child, we didn't have any law related to recycling, so many people just threw away recyclables with regular trash.
2) **과거 학교에서의 재활용**: However, in some areas, schools asked students to bring scrap paper for recycling education.
3) **과거 일부 품목 판매**: Also, some people used to sell empty bottles and scrap paper to a supermarket or a junk shop to make some extra cash.

## Q2. [15번 유형: 이웃 국가와의 관계에 영향을 준 사건] I would like for you to think about a specific historic event that has affected the relationship between your country and one of its neighboring nations. It could be a treaty signed between two countries, a cultural event or a visit of another county's minister or president. Tell me everything that happened in detail.

>### ==Korea-Japan trade dispute==
>- **사건 소개**: Japan removed Korea from trusted trade partners, export restrictions on chemicals/semiconductor materials → Korea removed Japan from whitelist
>- **반응 및 변화**: Korean consumers boycotted Japanese products (clothes, beer, cosmetics, films); ongoing economic conflict, relations got worse

### ==Answer==
1) **사건 소개**: I remember reading an article about a trade dispute between Korea and Japan. A few years ago, Japan removed Korea from its list of trusted trade partners and placed restrictions on the export of chemicals and semiconductor materials to Korea. As a response to this, Korea also removed Japan from the trade 'whitelist'. This actually became a Korea-Japan trade dispute.
2) **사건에 대한 반응 및 변화**: Since then, Korean consumers have boycotted Japanese products, including clothes, beer, cosmetics and even films. This dispute is still an ongoing economic conflict, and it has made the relations between the two countries get worse.
